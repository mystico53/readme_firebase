The app, whose name is pronounced "liss me," converts text-based content into audio format and adds these newly created audiobooks to a personalized playlist. This enables users to save articles for later listening, turning idle moments into opportunities for learning and staying informed.

"We created Lisme for the millions of people who want to stay up-to-date but find themselves short on time to read," said Konstantin, the app's co-founder and CEO. "Now, your commute, workout, or household chores can become productive listening time."

Users can save articles to their Lisme playlist by sharing links with the app. Lisme then uses advanced text-to-speech technology to create natural-sounding audio versions of the content, typically available within less than a minute.

Early adopters have praised the app's user-friendly interface and the quality of its audio conversions. "I've doubled my 'reading' since I started using Lisme," said Mark Thompson, a beta tester.

As people increasingly struggle to balance their desire to stay informed with busy schedules, Lisme may represent the future of content consumption. The app is available for download on Android devices, with a web version planned for release later this year.
